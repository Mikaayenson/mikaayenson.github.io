NIST Dependencies of Other Frameworks

According to Al-Ahmad and Mohammad, no single security framework addresses information security risks adequately. Organizations face challenges that hinder their risk analysis and try to adopt the best security practices of different frameworks in their assessments. Similar to NIST, all other frameworks have their strengths and weaknesses in the major functions to identify, protect, detect, respond, and recover. In the major function to identify within an organization, risk analysis and risk management strategies have several subcategories that NIST does not address solely. The NIST frame depends on other standards and frameworks like COBIT 5, ISO 27001, and ISA 62440 to provide information in sub-categories like identifying asset vulnerabilities, identifying threats, establishing risk management processes, determining risk tolerance, identifying business impacts, and several others. When NIST is limited in score, other frameworks like COBIT and ITIL are preferred since they are more general in nature. The purpose of the NIST framework was to provide the minimum level of information security requirements for government agencies (Schroeder, n.d.). The major strength of the framework is that it has a robust catalog of documentation and has a solid foundation since it has been developed overtime as technology has progressed.
